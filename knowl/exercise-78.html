Exercise 7.

Consider:
\begin{equation*} L_1 = \{ww | w \mbox{ is any binary string}\} \end{equation*}
It is known that \(L_1\) is not a context free language. \(0^*\) is a CFL and a RL. Since RLs are closed under intersection, \(L_1\) intersected with \(0^*\) must not be a RL.
Find the error in the proof above and describe it clearly.
