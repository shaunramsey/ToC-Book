Turing Machines accept languages based on "marking off" or reaching the end of input at an expected time. Heads are used to keep track of relevant inputs, and how they recieve their instructions is similar to what we have seen before. Think back to the "read pop push" structure that was introduced for PDAs. Heads move in similar ways on a "read move write" structure.
1 R \(\cancel{0}\)
\(\uparrow\) \(\uparrow\) \(\uparrow\)
read move write
If a head was given the instruction above, if it read in a 1 then it would move right but write over the 1 with a \(\cancel{0}\text{.}\)
