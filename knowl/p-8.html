A subset is simply a collection of things where those things are items that belong in another set. It is often denoted with the symbol \(\subseteq\text{.}\) Thus, \(\{a\}\) can be a subset of any other set that contains \(a\text{.}\) \(\{a,b\}\text{,}\) and even \(\{a,b,c\}\) are all subsets of \(\{a,b,c\}\text{.}\) In fact, \(\{a,b,c\}\) has quite a few subsets. Can you list all 8? So, a set, \(A\text{,}\) is a subset of a set, \(B\text{,}\) when all the elements in \(A\) are elements in \(B\text{.}\) We will write \(A \subseteq B\) to mean that \(A\) is a subset of \(B\text{.}\) In this case, \(A\) can have all the things of \(B\text{,}\) some of the things, or even none of the things of B. In the latter case if \(A \subseteq B\) and A has no elements of B in it, then A would also be \(\emptyset\text{.}\) Convince yourself that this is true.
