    Chapter 10 Pushdown Automata

    Objectives

    1. Introduction to Pushdown Automatas

    Section 10.1 Introduction to Pushdown Automatas

    The machines associated with CFLs are called Pushdown Automata (PDAs). They have memory in the form of an infinite stack.
    Pushing is adding a term to the stack while popping is removing a term from the stack. We use \(\Delta\) to indicate the end of input or the result of popping from an empty stack.
    To create PDA diagrams, we will lean on skills learned when drawing state machines. Instead of transitioning on a single character or a single character in a list, we will be introducing a notion that details what needs to read, what needs to be popped, and what needs to be pushed.
    0 \(\epsilon\) 0
    \(\uparrow\) \(\uparrow\) \(\uparrow\)
    read pop push
    We will further explore this notation in the exploration below.

    Exploration 6.

    We will walk through how to create a PDA from a CFL. Consider \(L_1\) where
    \begin{equation*} L_1 = 0^n1^n \end{equation*}
    The idea here is to push \(0\)s onto the stack until there are no more \(0\)s. We will then pop one \(0\) off of the stack for each \(1\) we read.
    Let’s consider this line of thinking in terms of steps and putting those steps into the read-pop-push notation from above.
    1. \(0\epsilon0\) - Read 0s, do not pop anything from stack, push a 0 to the stack
    2. \(10\epsilon\) - Read first 1, pop top 0 off of stack, do not push anything onto stack
    3. \(10\epsilon\) - Read rest of the 1s, pop top 0 off of stack, do not push anything onto stack
    4. \(\Delta\Delta\epsilon\) - Read in a \(\Delta\text{,}\) pop that \(\Delta\) off of stack, do not push anything onto stack
    We can then put these steps into a state diagram.

    Exploration 7.

    Now, try to create your own PDA for the following language:
    \begin{equation*} L_2 = \{a^i(bc)^i | i \geq 0 \} \end{equation*}
    It is easiest to first write out the necessary steps and then draw the PDA diagram.
    Here are the necessary steps:
    1. \(a\epsilon x\) - Read \(a\)s, do not pop anything from stack, push a \(x\) to the stack
    2. \(b\epsilon \epsilon\) - Read first \(b\text{,}\) do not pop anything from stack, do not push anything onto stack
    3. \(cx\epsilon\) - Read first \(c\text{,}\) pop first \(x\) from stack, do not push anything onto stack
    4. \(\Delta\Delta\epsilon\) - Read in a \(\Delta\text{,}\) pop that \(\Delta\) off of stack, do not push anything onto stack
    It is important to note that we must loop through checking for both the \(b\) and \(c\text{.}\)
    Answer.
